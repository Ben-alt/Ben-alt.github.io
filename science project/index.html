Natural Selection

Have you ever wondered how the millions of different species on earth came to be? Well the answer is through evolution, or how a species changes over time. One of the main ways that evolution occurs is through natural selection. But

What is natural selection?

Natural selection is the process through which species adapt to the environment around them. This means that an individual's features like color and size, also known as traits, benefits them in their environment. This is known as an advantageous trait. Examples of natural selection:

Giraffes used to be shorter. There would be variation in the giraffe's height, but all the food that was high up wouldn't be eaten by any other animal, so the giraffe's that were taller would get to eat more food. This means that the taller giraffe's would survive more often because they got to eat more food and this means that these giraffe's children would also be tall. This would repeat and over time the giraffe would become taller and taller.

Humans used something called pesticides to get rid of insects by killing them, but through natural selection insects can become resistant to pesticides. This is because only insects that are resistant to pesticides will live, allowing these insects to pass their traits that make them resistant to pesticides down to the next generation.

As you can see, natural selection has affected many animals and is a main reason to why we have so many different kinds and diverse animals on this planet.
